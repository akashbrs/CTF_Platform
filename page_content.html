\n \n \n \n Why Participate? \n

\n

\n Blindspot CTF is an exclusive, high-stakes Capture The Flag competition designed specifically for 2nd and 3rd-year students in the Department of Cybersecurity. This event bridges the gap between theoretical knowledge and real-world application, challenging you to uncover hidden vulnerabilities, exploit complex systems, and defend against advanced threats in a simulated adversarial environment. \n

\n
\n
\n
\n \n \n
\n
\n
\n

\n \n \n \n How It's Different \n

\n

\n Unlike standard entry-level competitions, we have engineered custom, scenario-based challenges tailored to your advanced curriculum. By focusing on "blindspots"ΓÇöthose easily overlooked security gaps in networks, web apps, and cryptographyΓÇöthis CTF pushes you to think like an elite adversary. It goes beyond simple puzzles, requiring multi-stage exploitation and deep, out-of-the-box critical thinking. \n

\n Rules & Regulations \n

\n

Please read and strictly adhere to the following guidelines.

\n
\n \n
\n
\n
    \n
  • \n \n No Flag Sharing: Collaboration outside of your assigned team is strictly prohibited. Do not share flags, hints, or solutions. \n
    • \n
  • \n \n No Infrastructure Attacks: Do not attack the CTF platform or infrastructure. This includes DoS/DDoS attacks, directory brute-forcing against the main site, or unauthorized scanning. \n
    • \n
  • \n \n Eligibility: Only registered 2nd and 3rd-year cybersecurity students from MRU are allowed to compete. Impersonation or using outside help is forbidden. \n
    • \n
  • \n \n No Flag Bruteforcing: Do not use automated tools to guess flags on the submission form. Rate limits are in place and violations will be logged. \n
    • \n
  • \n \n Learn & Have Fun: The primary goal of this CTF is education and skill development. Be respectful to fellow participants and admins! \n
    • \n
